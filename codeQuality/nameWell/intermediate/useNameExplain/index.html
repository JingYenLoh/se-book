Use Name to Explain

A name is not just for differentiation; it should explain the named entity to the reader accurately and at a sufficient level of detail.

Examples:

&lt;span class=&#39;glyphicon glyphicon-thumbs-down&#39; aria-hidden=&#39;true&#39;&gt;&lt;/span&gt; Bad &lt;span class=&#39;glyphicon glyphicon-thumbs-up&#39; aria-hidden=&#39;true&#39;&gt;&lt;/span&gt; Good
processInput() (what 'process'?) removeWhiteSpaceFromInput()
flag isValidInput
temp

If the name has multiple words, they should be in a sensible order.

Examples:

&lt;span class=&#39;glyphicon glyphicon-thumbs-down&#39; aria-hidden=&#39;true&#39;&gt;&lt;/span&gt; Bad &lt;span class=&#39;glyphicon glyphicon-thumbs-up&#39; aria-hidden=&#39;true&#39;&gt;&lt;/span&gt; Good
bySizeOrder() orderBySize()

Imagine going to the doctor's and saying "My eye1 is swollen"! Don’t use numbers or case to distinguish names.

Examples:

&lt;span class=&#39;glyphicon glyphicon-thumbs-down&#39; aria-hidden=&#39;true&#39;&gt;&lt;/span&gt; Bad &lt;span class=&#39;glyphicon glyphicon-thumbs-down&#39; aria-hidden=&#39;true&#39;&gt;&lt;/span&gt; Bad &lt;span class=&#39;glyphicon glyphicon-thumbs-up&#39; aria-hidden=&#39;true&#39;&gt;&lt;/span&gt; Good
value1, value2 value, Value originalValue, finalValue
